Heads up. This is a thought experiment, not a scientific study. Employment structure data comes from real statistical offices (see Karpathy's original repo), but all AI scores are LLM-generated estimates with zero peer review. The entire app was built in a single day using 20 concurrent AI agents working as a coding fleet — which should tell you something about the level of rigor involved. The scenarios explore what could happen, not what will. The author is well aware of both AI psychosis and AI doom narratives, but the real world has inertia. History consistently shows that technical revolutions create new possibilities we can't yet imagine. Or as Steve Yegge puts it: "We're all becoming a sort of Jeff Bezos. AI throws only the complex problems at us. It's exhausting." Fair point. This project is built with a deep belief in open source and human creativity. Please explore, enjoy, and don't take any of this too seriously.
